# Post-event recap post

**Timing:** 1–2 days after the event.
**Goal:** thank everyone who made the event happen, celebrate the night, and keep
the community warm for the next meetup.

Unlike the other post types, the recap is **warm and conversational**, with no
bold-Unicode header line. It is written as several **short stanzas separated by
blank lines** (one or two sentences each), opening with 🎉 and accenting with
🙌 / 💙. It points people to the **YouTube recording** of the talks — that link is
supplied by the user (ask for it; don't invent one).

## Structure (short stanzas, blank line between each)

1. 🎉 "That's a wrap" opening line.
2. Thank the speakers by name (tag them) and what they spoke about, plus a line of
   praise.
3. Thank the sponsor (tag them) and the host/venue (tag them).
4. A line celebrating the community / the evening's energy, ending with 🙌.
5. CTA to catch up via the link for anyone who missed it, ending with 💙.
6. The YouTube recording link (provided by the user).

## Template

```
🎉 That's a wrap on our latest ProductTank event!

A huge thank you to @Speaker 1 and @Speaker 2 for sharing their experiences on
<topic>. <One line of praise.>

Thanks also to our sponsor @Sponsor and host @Venue for making the evening possible.

Great conversations, thoughtful questions, and an energised product community as
always 🙌

Couldn't make it? Catch up via the link below and see you at the next one! 💙

<YouTube recording link — ask the user for this>
```

> The user must provide the YouTube link to the talk recording. If it isn't given,
> ask for it before finalising the post rather than leaving a placeholder.

## Real example (recap, ~2026)

> 🎉 That's a wrap on our latest ProductTank event!
>
> A huge thank you to Emma Burrows and Sean Jayakrishna for sharing their
> experiences on the transition from PM to founder. Great perspectives from both.

>
